UX Decision Journal

Documentation template for AI-related UX decisions. Built for compliance with EU AI Act, California SB 53, CCPA, and NIST AI RMF.

Purpose of This Template

This cheat sheet provides a precise, up-to-date reference for teams building digital products with AI capabilities. It helps systematically document UX decisions tied to AI features—supporting alignment with current regulatory expectations around transparency, risk assessment, and accountability. Documentation of this kind is widely recognized as a standard practice for demonstrating reasonable risk management.

Key Regulatory References (Official Sources)

European Union

EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)

Full official text with transparency, human oversight, and documentation requirements for high-risk AI systems.

Link to the source: eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj/eng

Key articles: 9–15 (risk management), 50 (transparency), 61 (conformity assessment). Most provisions apply from August 2, 2026.

California, USA

SB 53—Transparency in Frontier Artificial Intelligence Act (2025)

Requirements for frontier AI developers: safety frameworks, catastrophic risk reporting, and transparency disclosures.

Link to the source: leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/.../SB53

CCPA Regulations (2025 Updates: ADMT, Risk Assessments)

Notice, opt-out, and audit trail requirements for automated decision-making technology affecting California consumers.

Link to the source: cppa.ca.gov/regulations/ccpa_updates.html

Effective: January 1, 2026 (ADMT provisions phase in through 2027).

U.S. Federal (Voluntary Standard)

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0 + Generative AI Profile)

Widely adopted voluntary framework for managing AI risks—including traceability, documentation, and governance practices.

Link to the source: nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
